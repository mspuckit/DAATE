  • DAATE MVP Duration: January 19, 2022 - April 13, 2022
  • Challenges: Data Complexity, Legal Lingo, Creating Meaningful Visualizations
  • Lessons Learned: Subject Matter Experts (SME) and usability testing are critical to the success of a project, the criminal justice system is extremely complex.
  • Next Steps: Extend Work with Florida DOC, Add WA State Data, Add Sentiment Analysis, Co-ordinate with AEJG

DAATE MVP Challenges:

Throughout the development of the DAATE MVP the team encountered several challenges:

  • Data Complexity: Data from the Florida Department of Corrections (DOC) from 2004-2016 contained 1.3 million rows and 290 columns and we did not have access to a data dictionary. Because no data dictionary was available the team relied heavily on our subject matter experts.
  • Legal Lingo: Understanding the legal aspects (e.g. criminal codes) was a challenge for the team. To mitigate this and to narrow our focus for the MVP, the team chose three crime types to work with.
  • Creating Meaningful Visualizations: Due to the complexity of the data and the sensitivity of the topic, we build visualizations across several dashboards and relied on our subject matter experts for crucial feedback.

    • DAATE Next Steps:

    During the MVP process the team identified some areas for future improvement and focus. This includes:

  • Extend Work with the Florida DOC Data: Look at adding in counties, crime types and obtaining 2016-2021 data.
  • Extend Bias Detection to Prosecutors: DAATE is a tool intended to assist in identifying potential bias in the criminal system. This bias can take many different forms and can come from many different sources. Our MVP starts by looking at bias and judges and adding prosecutors is the next logical step given that 94% of cases end in plea bargain.
  • Add Washington State data: One goal of DAATE was to include data from as many states as possible. For our MVP we started with Florida but would like to extend to Washington State next applying the same modelling techniques.
  • Add Sentiment Analysis: In court interaction between legal parties and the defendant may play a role in sentencing. The DAATE team would like to explore this theory by applying natural language processing to court transcripts to determine sentiment analysis.
  • Co-ordinate with American Equity and Justice Group (AEJG): The DAATE team is inspired by the work of AEJG and would like to see if there is an opportunity to share learnings with the team at AEJG.
