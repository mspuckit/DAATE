The Use of the Judge Feature in our Models

Due to direct feedback from our subject maater experts, the predictive and causal modelling approaches have been run with and without judge information for comparison reasons.

We chose counties with enough judge information for our modelling purposes and noted that many of the judges names we misspelled, shortened or abbreviated. We have applied "fuzzy" search logic to group these variations in judges names together.

    • Causal Modelling:


    For causal modelling we focussed on robustness and applicability through a Quasi-experimental design: synthetic control, instrumental varibles and matching methods.

  • Approach:
    • Clustering based matching (unsupervised):
      • Generate data clusters based on feature proximity
      • Distance based clusters require Explainability for categorical features
      • Less situational
    • Evaluation:
      • Data Driven Method:
        • Elbow method
        • Empiral method
        • Intra cluster variance
      • Sensitivy Analysis
      • Confidence Interval and Significance test evaluation
    • Assumptions:
      • Stable unit treatment value assumption (SUTVA): Treatment of one unit does not affect the potential outcome of other units
      • Ignorability: Treatment assignment is independent of the potential outcome
    • Trade-offs:
      • Bias and Variance
      • Matching Explainability
      • Computation Efficiency
